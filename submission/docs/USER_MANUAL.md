# Turing — User Manual

TURING aims to resolve the gap for students to access an affordable, low-cost, and high-yield educational system by harnessing the processing of large language models and thereby assessing one's mathematical weaknesses and targeting weak spots with recommendations.

---

## Signing In

To begin using the application, users must first sign in with Google SSO.

1. Open the web app landing view and click either the "Get Started" button or the "Sign in with Google" option.
2. Complete the standard login prompt through the secure external Google account window.
3. Once the authentication process is verified, the system will automatically redirect you straight to your personal student dashboard workspace.

First-time users will complete a short onboarding flow to set their year level, school, course, and academic goal.

---

## Dashboard

Users are greeted with a clear dashboard that displays:

- **Projection Cascade** — your projected raw mark, HSC mark, and ATAR contribution, animated with peer band percentile.
- **Quick Stats** — questions completed, average score, and streak tracking.
- **Start Practising** button — jumps straight into generating your first question.

The sidebar on the left provides navigation between the main sections of the app.

---

## Practice (Home)

The main page allows users to practise mathematics problems with full AI support.

1. Use the progressive selection menu to choose a topic from the NSW syllabus (Advanced, Extension 1, or Extension 2).
2. Click "Generate Question" to receive a topic-specific problem rendered with proper mathematical notation (KaTeX).
3. Answer the question using one of three input modes:
   - **Draw** — sketch your working on the canvas with pen/eraser tools.
   - **Text** — type your answer directly, with LaTeX support for maths expressions.
   - **Upload** — capture or select a photo of handwritten working.
4. Click "Submit for Marking" to have your solution evaluated by AI.
5. Review your results — the AI returns a score, step-by-step breakdown of your working, correctness per step, and overall feedback.
6. Use the "AI Hint" button at any time to get targeted guidance without seeing the full solution.

A session timer tracks how long you spend on each question.

---

## Question Sets (Scanner)

The Question Sets tab allows users to:

1. Upload PDF worksheets (HSC past papers, textbook exercises, or custom worksheets).
2. The system automatically analyses uploaded sets, identifies topics, and extracts questions.
3. Launch a **Test Mode** (timed, no hints) or **Practice Mode** (untimed, hints available) session from any uploaded set.
4. View set-level completion scores and track progress across multiple worksheets.

---

## Topic Map

The Topic Map provides a 3D interactive visualisation of the entire NSW mathematics syllabus.

- Hubs represent Advanced, Extension 1, and Extension 2 courses.
- Sub-nodes represent individual topics, sized and coloured by your mastery level.
- Cross-links show prerequisite relationships between topics (e.g. Differentiation feeds into Further Calculus).
- Click and drag to rotate, scroll to zoom, and click any topic node to jump directly into practising that topic.
- Dim/grey nodes indicate topics you have not yet attempted.

---

## History & Sessions

The sidebar history panel stores all your past questions and attempts.

- Questions are grouped into **Chats** by session, so you can review an entire practice session at once.
- Click any past entry to reload that question and see your previous answer and score.
- Sessions can be deleted or reordered via drag-and-drop.
- History persists across sessions — log out and back in to find all your previous work intact.

---

## Settings

The Settings page is accessible from the sidebar and is organised into six tabs for managing every aspect of your Turing experience. The General tab lets you set your interface language (Australian or US English), choose which page opens on launch, and configure your default math input mode and LaTeX auto-correction preferences. The Profile tab displays your Google account details including your profile picture and email, and allows you to update your display name, institution, and HSC mathematics course enrolment, with a unique Turing academic identifier generated automatically for your account. The Appearance tab provides full theme control with dark, light, and system-follow modes, a font scale slider for adjusting text size from 80% to 150%, and accessibility toggles for reducing motion, enabling high contrast, and switching to a dyslexic-friendly typeface. The Notifications tab manages your email and browser push notification channels, along with optional daily study reminders and a weekly progress report delivered every Monday. The Privacy & Data tab gives you control over anonymous usage analytics, the option to share progress with your institution, the ability to export all your data including questions and history, and tools to clear your question history or permanently delete your entire account with all associated data. The Account tab shows your current plan tier with its feature set, and provides a sign-out-everywhere option to end all active sessions across devices.

## Getting the Most Out of Turing

- **Practise consistently** — the neural recommendation engine learns from your attempts and prioritises your weakest topics.
- **Use hints sparingly** — attempt the problem fully before requesting an AI hint to maximise learning.
- **Upload real worksheets** — the system analyses actual HSC-style problems and gives you realistic exam practice.
- **Watch your projection cascade** — track how your projected marks improve as you complete more questions.
- **Explore the topic map** — use it to identify prerequisite gaps before tackling advanced topics.
